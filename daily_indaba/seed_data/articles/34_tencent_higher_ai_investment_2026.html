Tencent Holdings has pledged to significantly increase its investment in artificial intelligence in 2026, including accelerating the development of its proprietary Hunyuan large language model, after acknowledging that US chip export restrictions constrained its capital expenditure plans in the previous year. The announcement came alongside strong fourth-quarter results that showed the Chinese technology giant's quarterly revenue rising 13 percent, driven by gaming and growing AI-related demand across its platform ecosystem.

Tencent President Martin Lau told reporters following the earnings release that the company aimed to substantially boost its capital expenditure in 2026, though he declined to specify the total amount. Digitimes reported that the company was planning to double its AI product investment from approximately 18 billion yuan to 36 billion yuan for the year, a signal of the pace at which Tencent is seeking to catch up after supply constraints slowed its 2025 hardware acquisition plans. The company's total capital expenditure reached about 79 billion yuan, or $11.49 billion, in 2025 — up from 77 billion yuan in 2024, but below internal targets because of difficulties in acquiring advanced AI chips due to US export controls on Nvidia's most powerful processors destined for China.

The US restrictions, which have been progressively tightened since 2022, have forced Chinese technology companies including Tencent, Alibaba, and Baidu to develop domestic alternatives or acquire allowed-tier chips in larger volumes. Tencent recently recruited former OpenAI researcher Yao Shunyu to lead the development of its Hunyuan model, a move that signals the seriousness with which it is treating its in-house AI capability. The company has framed its long-term competitiveness as depending on building deep AI infrastructure that is not vulnerable to external technology supply disruptions.

Tencent's gaming and cloud services divisions have both benefited from the wave of AI integration across its platforms, with advertisers paying premium rates for AI-optimised targeting tools and cloud customers increasing their usage of AI inference and training services. Revenue from the WeChat ecosystem, which remains central to Tencent's business model, showed continued growth as AI-enhanced features drove user engagement metrics upward. The company said its AI-generated content tools are now being used by a growing proportion of its advertising clients, reducing creative production costs and improving campaign outcomes.

Despite the strong results and confident tone on AI spending, Tencent's share price traded cautiously on the earnings day, with investors weighing the increased capex commitments against the company's track record of measured capital discipline. Analysts noted that a doubling of AI product investment, if delivered, would place significant pressure on profit margins in the near term, though most long-term forecasts remain constructive on the thesis that Tencent's AI platform buildout will drive superior monetisation over a three-to-five-year horizon. The company's position as the operator of China's most pervasive messaging and payments platform gives it an advantage in deploying AI at scale to hundreds of millions of users.
