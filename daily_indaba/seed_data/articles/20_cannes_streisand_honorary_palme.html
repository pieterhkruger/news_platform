The Cannes Film Festival has announced that Barbra Streisand will receive an Honorary Palme d'Or at this year's ceremony, recognising her decades-long contribution to cinema as an actress, director, producer and singer. Streisand, who is eighty-three years old, will become only the fourth person to receive the honour, joining a group that includes Jeanne Moreau, Jean-Luc Godard and Agnès Varda. Festival president Iris Knobloch described Streisand as a singular creative force whose influence on American film culture extended well beyond any individual performance or production.

Streisand's connection to Cannes stretches back to the 1970s, when several of her films received attention at the festival. Her directorial debut, Yentl, released in 1983, is considered one of the most significant directorial achievements in Hollywood history by a woman, and her follow-up The Prince of Tides earned seven Academy Award nominations. Despite the critical recognition those films received, Streisand was notably not nominated for an Oscar for either directorial effort, an omission that has since become a frequently cited example of historical gender bias in the awards establishment.

The announcement was welcomed by figures across the film industry, many of whom noted that Cannes providing this recognition was particularly meaningful given the festival's historical prestige and its role in shaping the global canon of cinema. Several prominent directors and actors took to social media to congratulate Streisand, with a number describing her as an inspiration who had expanded what was considered possible for women working behind the camera in an era when that path was actively discouraged.

Streisand is expected to attend the ceremony in person, her first public appearance at a major international film event in several years. The festival confirmed that a retrospective programme of her most significant films would run parallel to the main competition during the event. French culture minister Rachida Dati issued a statement welcoming the honour and praising Streisand's artistic legacy, noting that French audiences have long held her work in particularly high esteem.
