South Africa's manufacturing output fell 0.7 percent year on year in January, although the sector showed some month-on-month recovery. The figures suggest that factories are still operating in a difficult environment shaped by weak demand, cost pressures and uneven industrial momentum. Even so, the monthly bounce offers a limited sign that production conditions may be stabilising after a softer end to 2025. Business groups say the real test will be whether transport reliability, energy conditions and export demand can improve together. For now, the sector appears caught between short-term recovery signals and a still-fragile annual trend.
