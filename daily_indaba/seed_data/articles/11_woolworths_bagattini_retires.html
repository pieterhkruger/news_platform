Woolworths Holdings announced on Thursday that chief executive Roy Bagattini will retire from the role at the end of the current financial year, concluding a tenure that saw the group navigate the COVID-19 disruption, the sale of its Australian David Jones department store chain, and a significant restructuring of its South African food and fashion operations. The board confirmed that Sam Ngumeni, currently the group's chief operating officer and a twenty-year veteran of the company, will succeed Bagattini with effect from the new financial year.

Bagattini joined Woolworths as chief executive in early 2020, inheriting a business that was already under pressure from the underperformance of its offshore retail assets. His tenure was defined in large part by the decisive exit from David Jones, which had been a source of substantial write-downs and management distraction since its acquisition in 2014. The divestment freed up capital and management focus for the core South African operations, where the food business in particular has delivered consistent growth, outperforming many competitors in a challenging consumer environment.

Ngumeni, who will become one of the most prominent black executives leading a JSE-listed retail group, has been closely involved in operational strategy across both the food and fashion divisions. Analysts described the succession as orderly and noted that Ngumeni's institutional knowledge of the business reduces transition risk. The Woolworths board said the handover process would begin immediately, with Bagattini remaining available in an advisory capacity for a defined period after his formal departure.

Shares in Woolworths rose modestly on the announcement, which the market appeared to receive as a smooth and well-managed transition. Investors have generally rewarded the group's post-David Jones focus on its South African core, and the food division's resilient performance has provided a degree of insulation from the weakness in discretionary clothing and homeware spending that has affected the broader retail sector. Analysts said the priority for the incoming chief executive would be sustaining the food growth trajectory while driving a recovery in fashion and beauty.
