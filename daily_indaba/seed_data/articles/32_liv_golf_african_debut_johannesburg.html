LIV Golf made its long-anticipated debut on African soil at the Club at Steyn City in Midrand, on the outskirts of Johannesburg, on Thursday, with South African fans turning out in large and emotional numbers for what marked the biggest professional golf tournament to be held in the country in more than two decades. The event, the fifth event of the 2026 LIV Golf season, had been the subject of intense excitement since its announcement, with many South African supporters seeing it as a homecoming of sorts for the several prominent South African professionals on the league's roster.

The Club at Steyn City, a par-71 layout measuring 7,557 yards and set in the elevated Highveld terrain northwest of Johannesburg, hosted its first major professional event with course conditions described by players as firm, fast and demanding. The course, part of the vast Steyn City residential estate that stretches over approximately 2,000 acres, provided a dramatic and distinctive setting, with the Johannesburg skyline visible from several elevated vantage points along the back nine. LIV Golf described the venue as one of its most visually striking settings of the season.

South African players including Charl Schwartzel, Branden Grace, and the Stinger GC team — composed of South African and African golfers — were greeted with sustained applause and visible emotion from fans who had waited years for a major international golf event to return to their shores. Crowds draped in South African flags lined the fairways from the first tee, and there were scenes of celebration whenever any local favourite made a birdie or hit an exceptional shot. The noise levels and atmosphere were likened by some veteran caddies to a rugby international.

The tournament has a total prize purse of $30 million, with $4 million earmarked for the individual champion and $3 million to be split among the winning team. Beyond the prize money, the event's significance was felt in its economic contribution to the local area, with LIV Golf estimating significant hospitality and tourism spending over the four-day event. The local organising committee said that hotel occupancy across greater Johannesburg rose sharply in the week of the tournament, and the event generated considerable broadcast and social media attention across the African continent.

LIV Golf CEO Greg Norman described the South Africa debut as a milestone for the league's global ambitions and a vindication of its strategy to expand beyond the United States and Asia to reach new audiences. For many South African golf fans, the tournament was also an opportunity to see world-class golf on home soil without the expense of international travel. Demand for tickets exceeded supply within days of going on sale, and the gallery size on the first day was among the largest seen at any South African golf event in the modern era.
