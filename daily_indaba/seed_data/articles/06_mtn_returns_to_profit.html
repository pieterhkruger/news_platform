MTN Group has swung back to profitability, recording a profit after tax of R27.4 billion for the financial year ended 31 December 2025, compared with a restated loss of R10.9 billion in 2024. The turnaround, detailed in results released on Monday, marks a decisive recovery for the Johannesburg-listed telecommunications group after currency pressures and impairments weighed on prior performance. Key African operations drove the improvement, with MTN Nigeria delivering a major rebound and MTN Ghana posting robust growth. On a constant-currency basis, service revenue at MTN Nigeria rose 54.9 percent while MTN Ghana advanced 35.9 percent, underpinning the group’s overall service revenue increase of 22.7 percent to R218.5 billion. MTN South Africa showed more modest growth of 2.0 percent in constant-currency terms. Data and fintech businesses provided further momentum. Data revenue climbed 37.7 percent to R101.5 billion, reflecting accelerated traffic growth and rising average monthly usage per customer to 12.5 gigabytes. Fintech transaction volumes increased 15 percent to more than 23 billion. Group EBITDA before once-off items rose 64 percent to R98.5 billion, lifting the margin to 43.5 percent. The group surpassed its Ambition 2025 customer target, closing the year with more than 307 million voice subscribers, 172 million data users and 70 million Mobile Money customers across 16 markets. Capital investment of R38.5 billion supported network expansion, broadband coverage reaching 94 percent of the population served and a 14 percent reduction in average data costs. Shareholders will benefit from a 45 percent increase in the full-year dividend to 500 cents per share and a new R6 billion share buyback programme. MTN president and chief executive Ralph Mupita described 2025 as an “excellent” year that completed the Ambition 2025 strategy and positioned the group for its next phase of growth under the Ambition 2030 framework focused on connectivity, fintech and digital infrastructure.
