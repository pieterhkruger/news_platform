The war in Ukraine has intensified once more, with Russian authorities reporting what they describe as a major Ukrainian drone assault on Moscow and surrounding regions on 14 March 2026. Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin stated that air defence systems intercepted and destroyed at least 66 drones heading toward the capital, marking the largest such attack on the city this year according to Russian state media. The Russian Defence Ministry claimed that around 300 Ukrainian fixed-wing drones targeted various areas of Russia throughout the day, with 280 reportedly downed over regions including Bryansk, Kaluga, Belgorod, Tver, Smolensk, Kursk, Krasnodar Krai, and the Moscow oblast. The reported drone incursion comes amid a broader exchange of long-range strikes between the two sides. Ukrainian officials have not immediately commented on the Moscow claims, but Kyiv has increasingly employed drones to target Russian military logistics, energy infrastructure, and rear areas as part of its strategy to disrupt Moscow's war effort. Recent Ukrainian operations have included strikes on Russian ammunition depots, air defence systems, and transport assets in occupied territories and deeper into Russia, demonstrating growing capabilities in mid- and long-range unmanned systems. Russian forces, in turn, launched large-scale missile and drone attacks on Ukraine overnight into 14 March, killing at least six people and wounding others across multiple regions. President Volodymyr Zelenskyy reported that the strikes targeted energy infrastructure in the Kyiv region but also damaged residential buildings, schools, and businesses in Kyiv, Sumy, Kharkiv, Dnipro, and Mykolaiv oblasts. Ukrainian air defences downed most of the incoming threats—around 430 drones and 68 missiles—but some impacts caused power outages and civilian harm, with officials noting Russia's apparent use of the ongoing conflict involving Iran as cover for escalated bombardment. The tit-for-tat aerial campaign highlights the evolving drone warfare dynamic, where both sides seek to impose costs far from the front lines. Ukrainian advances in southern regions, including reported liberation of territory in Dnipropetrovsk Oblast, contrast with Russian claims of progress elsewhere, though independent assessments indicate limited territorial shifts overall. The intensified strikes coincide with stalled diplomatic efforts, raising concerns about further escalation as spring approaches and both militaries prepare for potential new offensives. With air defence systems under strain and civilian infrastructure repeatedly hit, the conflict's attritional nature continues to exact a heavy toll. International observers have called for renewed focus on de-escalation measures, though mutual accusations and ongoing military operations suggest little immediate prospect of respite on the ground or in the skies.
