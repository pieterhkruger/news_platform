About The Indaba

South Africa in Conversation

The Daily Indaba is South Africa's independent digital news platform built on the belief that honest, fearless journalism is not a luxury but a necessity for a functioning democracy.

Our Mission

We exist to connect readers, journalists, and publishers through reporting that holds power to account, amplifies under-heard voices, and reflects the full complexity of South African life. We do not serve a political party, a corporate interest, or a single community. We serve the story.

What "Indaba" Means

An indaba is a gathering: a forum where different voices come together to discuss matters of shared importance. It is a concept rooted in southern African tradition that values dialogue, deliberation, and the right of every person to be heard. We named this platform after that tradition because we believe good journalism is not a broadcast; it is a conversation.

Our Independence

The Daily Indaba is editorially and commercially independent. Our journalists are free to follow stories wherever they lead. Our editors apply a single standard: is it true, is it fair, and does it matter? We do not spike stories because they are inconvenient, and we do not pursue stories because they are politically useful. The platform carries no advertising of any kind: no banners, no sponsored content, no commercial partnerships. This is a deliberate policy: our editorial decisions are made by journalists and editors alone, and we intend to keep it that way.

Roles on the Platform

The Daily Indaba is built around four distinct roles. Each comes with specific responsibilities and corresponding access.

Readers

Readers are the heart of the indaba. Registered readers subscribe to individual journalists or publishers to unlock full access to their articles. Unsubscribed articles show only the headline and opening sentence; subscribe to read in full. Readers may post comments and reply to other readers on articles they have access to. Readers are expected to engage in good faith, with curiosity, civility, and a willingness to consider views that differ from their own.

Journalists

Journalists are the voice of The Daily Indaba. They write and submit articles for editorial review, create and manage newsletters, and build a body of work under their own byline. Articles submitted by journalists are not published immediately; they are reviewed by an editor before appearing on the platform. Journalists are held to a standard of accuracy, fairness, and transparency. Fabrication, plagiarism, and undisclosed conflicts of interest are grounds for removal from the platform.

Editors

Editors are responsible for the quality and integrity of everything that appears on The Daily Indaba. They review submitted articles, approve those that meet the platform's standards, and return those that require revision with clear, constructive feedback. Editors may also write and manage newsletters. They have oversight of the full article catalogue and may edit or remove any published piece if factual or ethical concerns arise. An editor's decision on publication is final.

Publishers

Publishers represent news organisations on the platform. They manage their public profile, assign registered editors to curate publisher-linked articles, and make their publications visible to readers who want to subscribe to trusted institutional reporting alongside independent journalism.

Comment Conduct

The comment section is a core part of what makes The Daily Indaba an indaba. We want robust debate. We want disagreement. We want readers who challenge each other and the journalists with evidence, argument, and passion. The rules below are therefore not about comfort; they are about maintaining the conditions in which genuine conversation can happen.

  • Engage with the argument, not the person. Criticise a view, a claim, or a policy as forcefully as you like. Personal attacks, insults, and targeted harassment are not permitted and will be removed.
  • Substantiate strong claims. Asserting that something is false is not the same as showing that it is. Readers who consistently spread demonstrably untrue information may have commenting privileges suspended.
  • Declare relevant interests. If you have a personal, professional, or financial interest in a topic you are commenting on, disclose it. The indaba values transparency.
  • No hate speech. Comments that dehumanise people on the basis of race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or any other protected characteristic will be removed immediately and the account suspended.
  • No incitement. Comments that encourage violence, illegal activity, or harm to identifiable individuals or groups will be removed and, where appropriate, referred to the relevant authorities.
  • Respect the thread. Stay on topic. Do not flood a thread with repetitive posts. Do not attempt to derail legitimate discussion through noise or provocation.
  • Respect editorial and community standards. If a comment is removed for violating platform rules, do not repost the same content or use the thread to harass other users.

Comments are limited in length to encourage precision. Readers who repeatedly breach these standards will have commenting privileges restricted or removed.

Platform Policies

No Advertising

The Daily Indaba does not carry advertising of any kind. There are no display ads, no sponsored articles, no affiliate links, and no commercial partnerships. This policy exists to protect editorial independence absolutely. The platform is sustained by its community, not by commercial relationships.

Editorial Transparency

Every article carries the name of its author and the date it was published. Corrections are published promptly and clearly marked. If an article is substantially updated after publication, this is noted. We do not delete published articles to avoid accountability; where serious errors are found, the article is corrected and the nature of the correction explained.

Data and Privacy

We collect only the information necessary to operate the platform. We do not sell user data to third parties. Reader activity, such as articles read and subscriptions held, is not shared with advertisers or data brokers, because there are none. Accounts may be deleted on request.

Corrections Policy

Accuracy is non-negotiable. Readers who identify factual errors in published articles are encouraged to contact us at corrections@dailyindaba.co.za. All substantive corrections are reviewed by an editor and published on the article itself. We do not quietly fix errors without acknowledgement.

Our Team

We are a small, dedicated team of journalists, editors, and contributors based across South Africa. You can meet our journalists on the Our Journalists page and explore the publishers who work with us on our Publishers page.

Want to get in touch?

For editorial enquiries, tip-offs, corrections, or general feedback, visit our Contact page.
