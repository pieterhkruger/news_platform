Eliud Kipchoge, the Kenyan distance running legend widely regarded as the greatest marathon runner of all time, made history on Sunday by completing his first competitive marathon on African soil at the Cape Town Marathon. The race, held on a course that winds through the city's waterfront, mountain passes and coastal roads, drew tens of thousands of spectators who turned out to witness the occasion. Kipchoge finished in a time of two hours, three minutes and forty-one seconds, a course record that also stands as the fastest marathon ever run on the African continent.

For Kipchoge, the Cape Town race carries significance beyond the clock. Having broken the two-hour barrier in Vienna in 2019 and set the official world record of two hours and thirty-five seconds at the Berlin Marathon in 2022, the forty-one-year-old has spoken publicly about his desire to inspire a new generation of African runners by competing in a race on the continent. His presence in Cape Town generated enormous excitement across the country, with athletics officials describing the event as a landmark moment for road running in South Africa.

Athletics South Africa chief executive Aleck Skhosana said the participation of a figure of Kipchoge's stature would help grow participation and investment in distance running at all levels. Cape Town's iconic scenery and well-organised road infrastructure have long made it one of Africa's most attractive marathon venues, and officials expect the event's profile to increase substantially following the weekend's performance. Several South African elite runners also competed, with Lungisa Mdedelwa finishing as the top local athlete in fourth position overall.

Kipchoge, who has spoken about the importance of running as a vehicle for social development, addressed a crowd of schoolchildren at a pre-race event in Khayelitsha on Friday. He described Africa as the spiritual home of distance running and said he hoped his Cape Town appearance would encourage more young people from the continent to pursue athletic ambitions. The Cape Town Marathon organisers confirmed they had already received a significant increase in international entries for next year's edition following the announcement of his participation.
