The 98th Academy Awards ceremony was held on Sunday night at the Dolby Theatre in Hollywood, with the French-language war drama One Battle After Another emerging as the dominant film of the evening, claiming five awards including Best Picture, Best Director for Jacques Audiard, and Best Original Screenplay. The film, which follows a group of soldiers through the psychological aftermath of combat across several decades and conflicts, had been the frontrunner throughout awards season and its victories confirmed a strong year for international and non-English-language cinema at the Oscars.

Ryan Coogler's American horror drama Sinners was the other major winner of the night, picking up four awards including Best Actor for Michael B. Jordan and Best Cinematography. The film, set in the American Deep South in the 1930s and drawing on themes of racial violence, music and supernatural folklore, had generated substantial critical acclaim and commercial success since its release earlier in the year. Jordan's win marked his first Oscar nomination and first victory, and his acceptance speech, in which he paid tribute to Coogler's vision and the film's Black creative team, drew a prolonged standing ovation.

The Best Actress award went to Demi Moore for her performance in The Substance, a body horror film that had divided audiences but earned widespread recognition for Moore's commitment to a physically and emotionally demanding role. Moore's win was received as a recognition of a career that has long been undervalued by the awards establishment, and her speech — in which she reflected on years of being told she was not a serious actress — was one of the emotional highlights of the evening.

The ceremony, hosted by Quinta Brunson, was widely praised for its pacing and tone, with several presenters drawing applause for avoiding the kind of protracted or self-congratulatory segments that have historically tested audience patience. South African viewers noted with interest that no South African productions were nominated in major categories this year, though several local industry figures were present at the ceremony in professional capacities. Industry bodies said the result underscored the need for continued investment in South African content development with international distribution ambitions.
